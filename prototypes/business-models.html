Software as a Service (SaaS)

Both

Description: Subscription-based access to cloud-hosted software

Revenue Source: Monthly/annual subscriptions

Key Metrics: MRR, CAC, LTV, churn rate

Use Case: Cloud software for business workflows

Examples: Salesforce, Slack, HubSpot, Zoom

Platform/IaaS (PaaS/IaaS)

B2B

Description: Cloud infrastructure or platforms for developers

Revenue Source: Usage-based pricing or subscriptions

Key Metrics: Usage rates, uptime, adoption

Use Case: Cloud platforms for app deployment

Examples: AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud

Marketplace

Both

Description: Connects buyers and sellers via a digital platform

Revenue Source: Transaction fees, listing fees, commissions

Key Metrics: GMV, take rate, liquidity ratio

Use Case: Connecting buyers and sellers

Examples: Amazon, Uber, Airbnb, Fiverr

Freemium

Both

Description: Core product is free advanced features are paid

Revenue Source: Upgrades from free to paid plans

Key Metrics: Conversion rate, retention, CAC

Use Case: Free core product with paid upgrades

Examples: Dropbox, Zoom, Notion, Free-to-paid conversion, user retention, CAC

Ad-Supported

B2C

Description: Free to users revenue from advertisers

Revenue Source: Ad impressions, clicks, data monetization

Key Metrics: Ad revenue, impressions, engagement

Use Case: Free content monetized by ads

Examples: Google, Facebook, YouTube, Ad revenue per user, impressions, engagement

E-commerce

Both

Description: Online sale of products direct to consumers or businesses

Revenue Source: Sales margins, fulfillment fees, premium listings

Key Metrics: CAC, AOV, conversion rate

Use Case: Online sale of physical products

Examples: Shopify, Amazon, Etsy

Subscription Box / Hardware+Software

B2C

Description: Physical product bundled with ongoing software or services

Revenue Source: Hardware sales + recurring SaaS

Key Metrics: Device sales, attach rate, MRR

Use Case: Hardware bundled with subscription services

Examples: Peloton, Tesla, Apple

Licensing / Enterprise Software

B2B

Description: Paid licenses to use proprietary software

Revenue Source: Perpetual or annual license fees, support

Key Metrics: Renewals, deal size, seat count

Use Case: Licensed software for enterprise operations

Examples: Oracle, SAP

Open Source with Paid Services

Both

Description: Free core product with monetized services or features

Revenue Source: Support contracts, premium features, hosting

Key Metrics: Community growth, conversion, contracts

Use Case: Free core with paid features or support

Examples: Red Hat, GitLab, Elastic

Data-as-a-Service (DaaS)

B2B

Description: Access to datasets or APIs for integration

Revenue Source: Subscriptions or pay-per-use

Key Metrics: API usage, freshness, segments

Use Case: APIs delivering real-time data

Examples: Clearbit, Crunchbase, Snowflake

AI-as-a-Service / ML Platforms

B2B

Description: Machine learning tools/models offered via API or platform

Revenue Source: Usage-based or enterprise subscriptions

Key Metrics: Inference volume, latency, accuracy

Use Case: APIs providing pre-trained AI models

Examples: OpenAI, Hugging Face, DataRobot

Productized Services

B2B

Description: Standardized tech services packaged like products

Revenue Source: Fixed-fee pricing per package or tier

Key Metrics: CAC, MRR, churn, delivery time

Use Case: Standardized service packages for businesses

Examples: Design Pickle, ManyPixels

Usage-Based / Pay-as-You-Go

Both

Description: Charges customers based on actual usage

Revenue Source: Consumption billing

Key Metrics: Usage, ARPU, COGS, margin

Use Case: Pay-per-use APIs or tools

Examples: Twilio, Stripe, OpenAI

Affiliate / Referral-Based

Both

Description: Earns fees for referring users to third-party products

Revenue Source: Referral fees, affiliate commissions

Key Metrics: CTR, conversion, CPA, LTV

Use Case: Revenue from partner or referral links

Examples: NerdWallet, Honey, Skimlinks

Crowdsourced / Community-Driven

Both

Description: Product or content created by a user community

Revenue Source: Ads, sponsorships, donations, premium features

Key Metrics: Contributors, content quality, DAU

Use Case: Platforms built on user contributions

Examples: Wikipedia, Reddit, Stack Overflow

Tokenized / Web3 (Blockchain-based)

Both

Description: Users transact with or earn crypto tokens

Revenue Source: Token issuance, transaction fees, staking

Key Metrics: Token volume, wallet growth, fees

Use Case: Crypto-based decentralized platforms

Examples: Uniswap, Axie Infinity, Helium

Licensing IP / White-Labeling

B2B

Description: Technology licensed or rebranded by other businesses

Revenue Source: Licensing fees, royalties

Key Metrics: License count, renewals, royalties

Use Case: Rebranded tech sold to other companies

Examples: White-label fintech platforms, AI SDKs

Hardware-as-a-Service (HaaS)

B2B

Description: Hardware leased with service/software integration

Revenue Source: Subscription or lease payments

Key Metrics: Utilization, ACV, churn, support costs

Use Case: Leased hardware with service layers

Examples: Cisco Meraki, Ring for Business

Microtransactions / In-App Purchases

B2C

Description: Small purchases made within apps or games

Revenue Source: Volume of small payments (e.g. $0.99)

Key Metrics: ARPPU, conversion, retention

Use Case: Small in-app digital purchases

Examples: Fortnite, Candy Crush, Roblox

Razor and Blade

Both

Description: Base product sold cheaply to drive consumable sales

Revenue Source: Consumables, add-ons, app store sales

Key Metrics: Adoption rate, attach rate, margin

Use Case: Low-cost hardware with recurring add-ons

Examples: Xbox + games, printers + ink, Oculus + apps

Franchise / Platform Franchise

B2B

Description: Licensing a business model to third parties

Revenue Source: Franchise fees, royalties

Key Metrics: Retention, royalties, onboarding

Use Case: Replicable business platforms for partners

Examples: Shopify (like-platform franchise)

Hybrid Models

Both

Description: Combining two or more business models

Revenue Source: Mixed—subscriptions, ads, usage fees, etc.

Key Metrics: Blended ARPU, upsell rate, retention

Use Case: Blended models with multiple revenue streams

Examples: LinkedIn, Notion
